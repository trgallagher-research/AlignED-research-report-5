The Tutor in the Transcript

Tim Gallagher

March 2026 — DRAFT

This page shows 1,165 paired teacher responses comparing how an LLM (GPT-5 Mini) and real human teachers respond to students in mathematics classrooms. Ten transcripts were sampled from the NCTE Transcripts dataset, a corpus of recorded U.S. maths lessons, across different data collection years and lesson lengths.

For each entry, you can see the full conversation history the LLM received as input, the student utterance being responded to, what the human teacher actually said next, and what the LLM generated in response.

What changed from the initial run

An earlier pilot used Gemini 3.1 Pro on 142 student turns with a minimal prompt ("You are a mathematics teacher. Respond to the student.") and a 10-word minimum filter on student utterances. Methodological review identified several problems with that approach:

  • The word filter excluded pedagogically important turns. A student saying "I don't understand" (3 words) was filtered out, even though the teacher's response to that utterance is exactly the kind of moment this study should examine. The filter has been removed. All student turns are now included.
  • The prompt framed the task as one-to-one tutoring. "Respond to the student" (singular) made it impossible for the model to redirect to another student, address the class, or use any multi-party discourse moves. The updated prompt tells the model it is in a classroom with multiple students and may take any action a teacher might take.
  • Human response matching was not validated. When multiple students speak consecutively before the teacher responds, the pipeline matched all of them to the same teacher response — but the teacher was only responding to one. Each turn is now tagged as a direct match (student turn immediately followed by teacher turn) or indirect match (other students spoke in between), so the comparison can be filtered appropriately.
