The Tutor in the Transcript

Tim Gallagher

March 2026 — DRAFT

This page shows 142 paired teacher responses from a pilot study comparing how an LLM (Gemini 3.1 Pro) and real human teachers respond to students in mathematics classrooms.

For each entry, you can see the full conversation history from the real classroom transcript that the LLM received as input, the student utterance being responded to, what the human teacher actually said next, and what the LLM generated in response.

The LLM was given no pedagogical instructions. Its system prompt was: "You are a mathematics teacher. Respond to the student." The transcripts come from the NCTE Transcripts dataset, a corpus of recorded U.S. maths lessons. Ten transcripts were sampled across different years and lesson lengths.

The purpose is to check whether the LLM's responses make sense given what it saw, and to compare them against what the teacher actually did in the moment.

This is Report 5 in the AlignED series. For all reports, visit AlignED Reports.
